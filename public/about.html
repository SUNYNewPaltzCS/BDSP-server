Big Data

Gathering , integrating, managing and analyzing large amounts of time and place specific quantitative information from diverse sources.

Big Data in the context of municipal government refers to the collection, management and analysis of large amounts of data, typically on elements of service delivery, that are specific to a time and location. The analysis of this data may be used for benchmarking and performance metrics, and also to identify patterns, trends, and associations to inform decisions to achieve a desired result. Utilizing Big Data allows municipalities to identify opportunities for innovation and more accurately predict fiscal and policy outcomes.

Small Places

All small governments in New York are fiscally limited – e.g. they are all subject to the 2% tax property cap.

Some are in real trouble - 15 Hudson Valley local governments score above 50% on the State Comptroller’s fiscal stress index: 2 cities, 5 villages, 5 towns and 3 counties. Lacking resources, they cannot afford to invest in existing out-of-the-box big data solutions.

Simply put, they cannot afford to save money.

Why?

Local Governments are often led and staffed by dedicated, talented people. But there are too few of them, they must act across a range of substantive areas, and they are not always professionally trained in all the technical aspects of public management. Hard pressed decision makers lack the time, energy, information, and knowhow to institute performance metrics, benchmark service delivery, and employ fine grained data analysis.

The Solution

Big Data for Small Places is a program of the Benjamin Center for Public Policy Initiatives that is specifically designed to increase small places’ capacity to develop and institute evaluation programs, collect and analyze data, and to make more informed decisions.
