Testing in Poughkeepsie

The Benjamin Center worked with the City of Poughkeepsie to beta test the preliminary Big Data for Small Places platform

The app automatically tracks and maps the time and the location of sanitation vehicles, and by the use of inexpensive Android phones provided to personnel on each vehicle, allows for the manual entry of the tonnage of solid waste dropped off at the County Resource Recovery Agency.

The Commissioner will be able to add staffing information - who was on each truck each day - and the tipping fee. A Dashboard will then allow him to quickly look at how much refuse is collected, from where and when, and generate performance metrics, for example the operating cost for each run and the tonnage per hour of operation.

Newburgh Interactive Map

The Benjamin Center worked with the ESPRI Newburgh Anit-Poverty Initiative and the United Way of the Dutchess-Orange Region

As a part of Phase I of the ESPRI Anti-Poverty Initiative in Newburgh, the Benjamin Center created an online interactive mapping application that utilized a database of community assets to connect people in need with service provider that can assist them. The map can be found at www.newburghmap.com.
